01 · Chrome & Identity

Announcement Bar, Wordmark, Header & Mega Menu

The components fixed at the very top of this page are live. Hover Women or Collections in the navigation to open the editorial mega menu. This is the permanent frame the bride sees on every page — it must feel like the brass doors of a boutique, not a toolbar.

The Announcement Bar

A slim espresso ribbon carrying one quiet message at a time, rotating gently. It speaks of service — worldwide shipping, private appointments, the atelier's WhatsApp — never of discounts or urgency.

Luxury Rationale

Houses whisper. A single rotating line in warm champagne on near-black reads as concierge service, not a sale sticker. No countdowns, no red, no exclamation marks.

The Wordmark

The gold monogram is retired. The house now speaks through a typographic wordmark — KARISHMA & ASHITA in letter-spaced Bodoni capitals, ink on light and ivory on dark, its only flourish a single italic ampersand. A compact K&A serves mobile and favicons.

A letter-spaced wordmark is the most flagship signal of a maison — Celine, Saint Laurent, Sabyasachi. Engraved capitals read as couture and heritage; a logo image reads as a brand badge.

The Discipline of the Metal

The metal never fills a large area. It is a thread, a rule, the edge of a card — the way real bronze and zari appear in couture: precious because it is rare. Desaturated to a patina rather than a polish, it reads as heirloom, not costume. Bright accent colour is forbidden; the only “colour” is the photography itself.

Luxury Rationale

The Didone serif carries editorial authority and high-fashion contrast; the geometric sans carries calm. Wide letter-spacing slows the eye — the typographic equivalent of a hushed showroom. Two families, three weights, and italic reserved for the names of collections. Nothing decorative, nothing bridal.

The Spacing System

White space is the most expensive material in the house. An 8-point rhythm scales into generous, architectural intervals — sections breathe at 128–160px on desktop, so that every garment is given the room of a gallery wall.

05 · Foundation

Container Widths

Four measured frames govern every page — from full-bleed cinematic imagery to a narrow, book-like measure for storytelling. Content is never wider than the eye can comfortably read.

Full bleed · 100vw — hero film & campaign imagery
--c-wide · 1600px — editorial galleries & mega menu
--c · 1280px — default content frame
--c-narrow · 1040px — focused composition
--c-text · 680px — long-form reading
Luxury Rationale

Gutters scale from 20px on mobile to 64px on large screens. The frame should feel like the margin of a couture lookbook — never edge-to-edge text, never cramped.

10 · Detail

Lines, Corners & Light

The smallest decisions create the most luxury. Hairline rules instead of heavy borders. Square corners instead of rounded. Almost no shadow — depth is created by space, not by drop-shadows. Shadow appears only when something must float above the page.

Corners & Radius

Why Square

Couture is architectural. Sharp corners read as tailoring and gallery framing; rounded corners read as software. We permit only a 2px softening on inputs for comfort.

Shadow & Elevation

--none · cards rest flat on the page
--shadow-soft · subtle lift
--shadow-overlay · menus & drawers
Luxury Rationale

Heavy shadows are the fingerprint of cheap web design. Our cards have none — they are separated by white space alone. A single, large, low-opacity shadow is reserved for things that genuinely float: the mega menu, the cart drawer, the search overlay.

11 · Behaviour

Motion & Hover

Everything moves slowly. Transitions run 400–900ms on a soft ease, the tempo of fabric settling. Nothing bounces, nothing spins. Motion is used to reveal beauty, never to demand attention. Hover any element below to feel the house's pace.

Page Transition Philosophy

Between pages, the canvas performs a soft cross-fade — content fades up while the previous view fades to Bone. No white flash, no hard cut. Navigation should feel like turning the page of a lookbook: deliberate, continuous, hushed.
